About

I'm a MS student in Electrical & Computer Engineering at Duke University, advised by Dr.Ricardo Henao and Dr.Jichun Xie. I work with the pathologists from Department of Pathology at Duke health on AI systems that can be embedded into real clinical workflows and develop cross-modality foundation models to assist clinical reasoning.

Before Duke, I completed my B.S. in Information Management & Systems at Nankai University, where I received solid training in programming, data systems, and product / UI design, and spent irreplacable 4 years singing in NKChoir directed by Prof. Meng.

"A journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step."

Research Interest

Broadly, my research interests include:

AI4Health Clinical Reasoning & Decision support Computational Pathology Medical Imaging Multimodal Modeling Uncertainty-aware & Interpretable ML

Long term, I aim to develop AI systems that bridge algorithmic intelligence and real clinical practice, enabling pathologists and clinicians to make safer and more efficient decisions.
